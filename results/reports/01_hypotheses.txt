Stage: Hypotheses

H1. Supervised ensemble models will outperform simpler anomaly detection baselines on standard fraud classification metrics when labeled proxy data is available.
H2. Metrics suited to imbalanced learning, especially recall, F1-score, ROC-AUC, and PR-oriented interpretation, will be more informative than accuracy.
H3. Even when supervised models achieve stronger overall performance, anomaly-based methods remain useful for surfacing novel suspicious patterns in future waqf transaction monitoring.
H4. The proxy-data setting can support method selection and evaluation design, but cannot eliminate external-validity risk for deployment in real waqf systems.
